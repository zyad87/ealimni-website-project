Radiation therapy

Radiation therapy is basically X-rays given in high doses and energies, leading to the destruction of the target. These high energy X-rays are generally given in multiple small sessions to reduce the side-effects of the treatment and allow normal tissues to heal.

Radiation therapy is generally given on a daily basis 5 days a week for a few weeks. However, in certain situations the treatment is given as higher doses over a shorter number of sessions (typically 1 to 5) which is called Stereotactic Radiotherapy or Radiosurgery.


Does radiation therapy cause pain?

No it does not. The patient will not see or feel the X-rays during treatment. The X-rays will not burn the patient, and they are not similar to laser therapy. All of these are typical misconceptions of Cancer therapy by radiation

Radiation Therapy Steps

1-Clinic Visit

medical evaluation and sign the consent form.

2-CT simulation

Radiotherapist use CT scan with immobilization to prepare for treatment position.

3-Target Contouring

Radiation oncologists mark and outline the targeted area and organs at risk on the CT scan.

4-Dose calculation and quality assurance

Medical physicists use software with algorithms to calculate the radiation dose and ensure the treatment plan's quality.

5-Radiotherapy start

Radiotherapist deliver the high quality planned treatment by the radiotherapy machine.
